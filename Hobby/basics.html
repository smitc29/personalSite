Old-Timey Map using Photoshop

The follow project is a relatively simple type of image that you can make in Photoshop to fiurther develop your skills, and allows an immense range of flexibility in art styles, as it is designed to look like a drawing that has faded over time.

Part One

Start by making a new document in photoshop. Set the dimensions to something reasonable or for whatever purposes you might use the image for after making it, such as setting it to wallpaper dimensions or screen size.

Part Two

Always create a new layer when stating s new step or adding a new color. This allows you to edit each color seperately and allows large mistakes to be undone even after making more progress.

Part Three

Find or scan a texture of older, used paper that might have some stains or damage on it. Add this to a layer under your lines, allowing you to edit th eopacity of either layer as you deem necessary.

Part Four

Begin to add color to the draw on seperat elayers. I suggest using layers beneath the paper texture to make it seem like the paper itself is textured, and allowing the colors to receive a slight tint from the paper texture instead of vice-versa.

Part Five

Continue to add color until the majority of your drawing has color. Remember to make different layers for different colors to reduce damage when making mistakes.

Part Six

Add shadows, lighting, and further details as you deem appropriate. The amount of detail you add at this point is entirely up to you, and allows a good amount of flexibility. You can always edit your original line drawings at any point, so you should be good to keep moving forwards.
